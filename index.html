Design and Development

Career Timeline

'07

  • Obtained an associate's degree including a semester studying Graphic Design.
  • Purchased a used book for a few bucks to learn HTML, and built my first website using table-based HTML, then rebuilt it using CSS once I realized why that book was just a few bucks (it was terribly outdated).
  • Continued to learn, adding PHP, MySQL, and Javascript to my curriculum.
  • Photoshop time, getting familiarized with the pen tool to cut out lots and lots of hats from their background in one of my first contract positions!

'08

  • Intro to CMSs and Firebug. Whoa, tools!
  • Full swing into my Freelance career - web design, maintenance, data entry, and converting static mockups into pixel-perfect HTML/CSS, slowly mastering CSS and cross-browser compatibility.
  • Gained a feel for what works and what doesn't while designing websites and interfaces for blogs, small businesses, and startups.

'09

  • Dug into custom Wordpress theming. Developed a simple starter theme which I built on for my own personal projects and for new clients - skills which expanded into tackling tough theme and plugin customizations for clients, and for more obscure CMSs.
  • Remember that table-based HTML I mistakenly learned? Came in handy when creating custom email newsletter templates that worked in various email clients (Outlook, anyone?).
  • Got deeper into UI/UX design doing work for startups. Discovered an ability to pinpoint areas of confusion on a site and improve usability using information architecture, clear language and communication, layout and design.
  • Wrote, modified, refactored, and integrated HTML/CSS on larger, more complex projects. Began working more closely with engineers. Introduction to version control. Discovered a strength in writing clean code fast and efficiently, and integrating HTML/CSS with various templates in PHP, ASP.NET, Wordpess, Drupal, and more.

'11

  • Began working with an agency serving mid-size businesses. Developed organizational skills and an ability to balance design, development, timeline, budget, and client needs and preferences.
  • New challenge: Responsive Design! I became the go-to-gal for converting a regular website to a responsive one.
  • After finding patterns among design and development processes and solve similar problems over and over again, I started putting more thought into documenting, and abstracting my code for easy re-use and customization for future projects.

'14

  • Got my first full-time job at Learning A-Z. Goodbye freelance, hello benefits!
  • Working on a larger team, I gained a new understanding of the importance of documentation and maintainability, and more in-depth experience with version control.
  • Developed an obsession for documentation, structure, and consistency, resulting in the creation of a styleguide to kick off an ongoing team HTML/CSS codebase cleanup. Thought more about color pallettes, visual patterns, naming conventions, and architecture than ever before.
  • Gained more expertise in UX design, creating wireframes for new features and overhauling existing ones. Sketching with OneNote on a Surface Pro and Adobe XD became my wireframing/prototyping tools of choice. Also attended NNG User Experience training in New York.

'16

  • With the support of my company, went back to school! During my year at the University of Michigan, I learned better documentation using RMEs, got very comfortable with functions, loops, arrays, abstraction using Classes, linked lists, recursion, binary trees, and memory management.
  • Continuing the journey! Deep dive into modular CSS using BEM, integration with Angular, refactoring legacy code, and establishing visual design standards. Examining complex challenges in Information Architecture. More wireframes and prototyping, user testing, and always obsessing about whitespace, alignment, color usage, and consistency, and balancing design, development, and business needs.
